Admissions Questions

Common inquiries about applying to EduPlus

Our undergraduate admission requirements include:

  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Minimum GPA of 2.5 (on a 4.0 scale)
  • Official high school transcripts
  • SAT/ACT scores (optional for 2023-2024)
  • Personal statement (500 words)

Some programs may have additional requirements. Please check the specific program page for details.

International students follow the same application process as domestic students with these additional requirements:

  • Proof of English proficiency (TOEFL/IELTS)
  • Evaluation of foreign transcripts
  • Financial documentation
  • Copy of passport

Our international admissions team is available to guide you through the process.

Application deadlines vary by program and semester:

  • Fall Semester: August 15 (Domestic), July 1 (International)
  • Spring Semester: December 15 (Domestic), November 1 (International)
  • Summer Semester: April 15 (Domestic), March 1 (International)

Some programs may have earlier deadlines. We recommend applying as early as possible.

Yes, we accept transfer credits from accredited institutions. To have your credits evaluated:

  1. Submit official transcripts from all colleges attended
  2. Provide course descriptions/syllabi if requested
  3. Wait for evaluation (typically 2-3 weeks)

Courses must be comparable to ours in content and quality, with a grade of C or better.

Admission decisions are typically made within 2-4 weeks after we receive all required documents. The process includes:

  • Initial document verification (1 week)
  • Committee review (1-2 weeks)
  • Decision notification (within 1 week of decision)

Some programs with additional requirements (like portfolios or interviews) may take longer.

Course Questions

Information about our programs and curriculum

We offer a wide range of courses across several disciplines:

  • Technology: Computer Science, Data Science, Cybersecurity, Web Development
  • Business: Business Administration, Marketing, Finance, Entrepreneurship
  • Design: Graphic Design, UX/UI, Digital Media
  • Health Sciences: Public Health, Healthcare Administration
  • Humanities: Creative Writing, Communications

We also offer professional certificates and continuing education courses.

Yes, we offer three types of course formats:

  1. On-Campus: Traditional in-person classes at our Boston location
  2. Online: Fully remote courses with flexible scheduling
  3. Hybrid: Combination of online and occasional in-person sessions

Many of our programs can be completed entirely online. Check individual program pages for format availability.

Course duration varies by program and format:

  • Undergraduate Courses: 15 weeks (semester system)
  • Graduate Courses: 12 weeks
  • Professional Certificates: 8-16 weeks depending on program
  • Bootcamps/Intensives: 4-12 weeks

Summer sessions and accelerated formats may have different timelines.

Yes, we offer several options for non-degree students:

  • Individual Courses: Take single courses for credit or personal enrichment
  • Professional Certificates: Short-term programs focused on specific skills
  • Audit Options: Attend courses without receiving credit (lower tuition)

Many students start with individual courses before committing to a full program.

We maintain small class sizes to ensure quality instruction:

  • Undergraduate Courses: 20-30 students
  • Graduate Courses: 15-20 students
  • Seminars/Workshops: 10-15 students
  • Online Courses: 25 students maximum

Some introductory lectures may be larger, but break into smaller discussion sections.

Financial Aid Questions

Information about tuition, scholarships, and payment options

Tuition varies by program and student status:

  • Undergraduate: $450 per credit hour
  • Graduate: $650 per credit hour
  • Professional Certificates: $1,200-$3,500 per program
  • Non-Credit Courses: $300-$800 per course

Most full-time students take 12-15 credit hours per semester. Additional fees may apply.

We offer several financial assistance options:

  • Merit Scholarships: Awarded based on academic achievement
  • Need-Based Grants: For students with demonstrated financial need
  • Federal Student Aid: Available to eligible students (FAFSA required)
  • Work-Study Programs: Part-time employment opportunities
  • Payment Plans: Monthly installment options

Over 75% of our students receive some form of financial assistance.

Our scholarship application process is straightforward:

  1. Complete the general admission application
  2. Submit the scholarship application form (available in your student portal)
  3. Provide any required supplemental materials (essays, recommendations, etc.)
  4. Meet application deadlines (typically 2 months before semester starts)

Some scholarships have specific criteria. Our financial aid office can help identify opportunities.

Yes, we offer flexible payment options:

  • Semester Plan: Pay 50% at registration, 50% at midterm
  • Monthly Plan: 4-5 equal payments throughout the semester
  • Deferred Plan: For students expecting financial aid disbursement

There is a small enrollment fee for payment plans. Contact the bursar's office to set up a plan.

International students have several financial aid options:

  • Merit Scholarships: Available based on academic credentials
  • Country-Specific Awards: For students from certain regions
  • Private Loans: With a qualified US cosigner
  • On-Campus Employment: Up to 20 hours/week while school is in session

International students typically aren't eligible for US federal aid but may qualify for institutional aid.
